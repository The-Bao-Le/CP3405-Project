## 3. Sprint Retrospective & Key Takeaways

Reviewing the historical log of this 5-day cycle highlights two primary operational bottlenecks that the team must proactively address in subsequent sprints:

1. **Infrastructure and Dependency Mitigation (Infrastructure Blockers)** The delayed rollout of the GitHub repository and collaborator permissions severely throttled the team’s version control workflow from Day 2 through Day 4, creating an artificial dependency bottleneck.
   * **Action Item for Next Sprint:** Infrastructure readiness (repository setup, tool access, and workspace provisioning) must be designated as a Priority-0 (P0) task on Day 1, with a dedicated owner to drive immediate resolution.
2. **End-of-Cycle Task Concurrency (Deadline Mitigation)** Day 5 inherited an excessive volume of concurrent activities—including template corrections, data synchronization, slide creation, speaker notes formulation, and branch merging—which introduced unnecessary delivery risk right before the 23:59 deadline.
   * **Action Item for Next Sprint:** Implement a strict "Internal Milestone Split." All individual role deliverables (R1–R8 equivalent) must be frozen 24 hours before the final deadline, leaving the final day exclusively for slide integration, visual polishing, and dry runs.
