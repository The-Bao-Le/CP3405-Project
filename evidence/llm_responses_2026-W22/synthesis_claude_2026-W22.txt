Friday's Core PCE Inflation report is the single most consequential event of the week. Treasury yields remain the primary macro headwind for equities, meaning a hotter-than-expected print would likely push the 10-year yield back toward the 4.70% invalidation threshold and trigger a broad risk-off move. A soft or in-line reading would reinforce the soft-landing narrative supported by falling oil prices and stable Fed expectations, providing a constructive backdrop into month-end.

**Most Important Technical Signal**

The S&P 500 holding above both the 8-day and 21-day EMAs is the most important technical condition this week, confirming the short-term uptrend remains intact. The Nasdaq 100 carries the highest confidence rating (Medium-High), reflecting technology's continued relative strength. Key SPX support sits at 7,330; resistance at 7,600. The overall technical verdict is Flat to Up, as momentum may be extended after recent record highs.

**Most Important Seasonal Factor**

The midterm election year cycle is the dominant seasonal context, placing Q2–Q3 in a historically weak period before a typical Q4 recovery. The Memorial Day week bearish tendency reinforces this caution. These headwinds are partially offset by the week-after-options-expiration bullish pattern and the ongoing Technology seasonal long window. Net seasonal read is cautious bearish-neutral, with technology as the lone clear seasonal positive.

**Final Weekly Call**

The S&P 500 is set up for a sideways-to-modestly-higher week, with the path of least resistance leaning up as long as the EMA structure holds and Friday's Core PCE does not surprise to the upside. Macro, technical, and seasonal inputs all carry simultaneous bullish and bearish offsets, which argues against a high-conviction directional call. The week is best framed as a PCE binary event wrapped in constructive technicals: bulls need a soft inflation print to unlock upside toward 7,600; bears need a hot print and a yield spike above 4.70% to validate seasonal and macro caution. Until Friday, price action is likely to be contained and rotational.
