## Evidence Summary

### Macro Evidence

The macro view was **Neutral-Bullish with Binary Risk**.

Bullish factors included falling oil prices, stable Federal Reserve expectations, and supportive market conditions.

Bearish risks included elevated Treasury yields, inflation uncertainty, and geopolitical risk.

The most important macro driver was the Friday Core PCE Inflation Report because inflation data could affect Treasury yields and Federal Reserve expectations.

### Technical Evidence

The technical report showed that SPX and NDX remained bullish because they stayed above key support levels.

IWM had a more cautious **Flat → Up** view because small-cap stocks remained more sensitive to interest rate movements.

Overall, the technical view supported a cautiously positive market outlook.

### Human Score Evidence

The Human Score helped the team avoid relying only on AI outputs.

The team used Human Score to compare AI consensus with market evidence and make the final forecast more evidence-based.

## What Worked

The team correctly identified that SPX and NDX still had positive momentum. Both indices finished slightly positive, which supported the neutral-to-bullish view.

The technical evidence was useful because it showed that major indices remained supported.

The macro evidence was also useful because it warned the team not to become overconfident due to inflation risk and elevated Treasury yields.

---

## What Did Not Work

The team underestimated small-cap weakness.

IWM did not recover as expected and finished negative. This means the team’s forecast was better for large-cap and technology-related indices than for small-cap stocks.

The AI consensus was useful, but it was not enough by itself. The team needed to give more attention to market breadth and whether the rally was broad or concentrated.

---

## What We Learned

The main learning is that market prediction should not only focus on whether the market goes up or down. The team also needs to check whether the confidence level is reasonable.

This week, SPX and NDX moved slightly higher, but IWM moved lower. This shows that the market was not equally strong across all areas.

Small-cap weakness was the main issue we missed.

R8’s updated actuals showed that market breadth was uneven. Utilities, Communication Services, and Technology led the market, while Energy, Financials, and Materials lagged.

This confirms that the market was selective rather than broadly bullish. For Sprint 2, we should check sector rotation and market breadth before trusting a bullish AI consensus.

---

## Next Sprint Improvement

In Sprint 2, the team will not rely only on AI consensus.

We will compare AI consensus with the Human Score before finalising the forecast.

We will also give more weight to:

1. Market breadth
2. Small-cap weakness
3. Sector rotation
4. Whether leadership is broad or concentrated
5. Macro risks from yields and inflation
