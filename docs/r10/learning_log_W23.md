## Key QA Observations
The strongest agreement this week comes from the LLM comparison and Human Score. All four AI models predict Down with a Neutral-Bearish bias and Medium confidence. The Human Score is also -3, which supports the same cautious view.

The expected SPX range from the LLM comparison is 7,360–7,420. This gives the team a clear prediction range instead of only a general direction.

## Team Learning
The main learning this week is that strong AI agreement is useful, but it should not be treated as the final answer by itself. Even when all four AI models agree, the team still needs to compare AI consensus with Human Score, technical structure, macro risk, almanac evidence, and human judgment.

## One Change for Next Sprint
For the next sprint, the team should complete all agent outputs before running the final AI synthesis. This will make the LLM comparison more reliable and reduce the risk of copying AI consensus without checking the evidence first.

## R10 Final Learning Statement
The final prediction should not simply copy AI consensus. It should be checked against Human Score, technical evidence, macro risks, and market context before submission.
